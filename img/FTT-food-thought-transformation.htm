Wellness

Wellness is our Individual Responsibility

Although wellness seems to be very trendy at the moment it is indeed a model with ancient roots in civilizations from the East to the West, these civilizations include Chinese, Indian, Greek, and Roman. In Europe and the United States, a vast intellectual, religious, and natural medicine movement developed in the 19th century. Their focus was self-healing and prevention, using natural approaches.

Continue Reading...

It is important to point out that wellness is our individual responsibility, it is related with our actions, intentions, choices, daily practices and what we prioritise in our lives. Wellness is not a state of being, such as being happy or healthy and such, and should not be confused with wellbeing or happiness.

Wellness is when you actively choose to live a holistic/ healthy lifestyle that will promote overall health and wellbeing in at least six dimensions. These are not just about your physical health, wellness models include:

  • Mental: Self-development; through learning, dealing with trauma, codependency, self-healing, creativity and more
  • Spiritual: Finding your connection with source, purpose and meaning in Human existence, connection with self, with others and the planet
  • Emotional: Being mindful of your own feelings, thoughts, and belief systems, being acceptant of what is and able to express your own ideas without fear or judgement
  • Environmental: Awareness of the role we play in keeping our environment safe, whether by taking care of the planet and being responsible of our actions, or the people we surround ourselves with, the places we go, the job we have
  • Social: Being connected to others, interact and sense of belonging to a community
  • Physical: Nutrition, sleep hygiene, exercise, meditation

You can count with our team of coaches at FTT-Lifestyle to guide you into all areas of wellness, whether via our 10 weeks courses or our 1-2-1 sessions.

Mental-health

Take Care of your Mental Health

Just because you do not have a diagnosed mental illness it does not mean you should not take care of your mental health. We all should and need to do so. Mental or emotional health is ever changing and related with your nutritional habits, lifestyle and the work you put into self.

Continue Reading...

We all have trauma in our life that possibly we did not even stop to address it, in the sense of healing. And how do we heal? By feeling the feelings without judgement, by mindfully analysing the situation and taking the time to go deep within.

How often, if ever, do you take time just to face the root cause of your emotions? Do you journal regularly? Do you have a support system? Do you analyse your behaviours and choices at the end of each day?

Do you take time for self-care, such as a long bath, read a book, meditate, exercise? Are you aware that your diet has a major impact in your mental health?

The key features of mental health include our emotional, psychological and social wellbeing, and has a major impact on how we feel, think, act and react. It also affects our choices, the way we relate with self and others and how we perceive and respond to stress or stressful situations.

Nutrition

The FTT-Lifestyle team utilises Nutrition as a basis to bring your body functioning up to homeostasis as well as your mental health, amongst other natural therapies and coaching.

Nutrition is related to all that you think, eat and drink, how it is chewed-digested, absorbed, assimilated, as well as its biosynthesis, catabolism, and excretion. What you consume is directly involved in the maintenance, growth, repair, reproduction, and health of your body and mind.

Diet is what an individual consumes on a regular basis and a balanced diet is to consume a correct amount of good quality and natural nutrients that assist in nurturing your cells and assist with your body’s systems to enable a good functioning of body and mind.

Mineral Deficiency and Cultural Foods- The idea behind FTT Lifestyle is to allow people to understand that cultural habits and cultural foods may contribute to mineral deficiency. Cultural habits and cultural foods are those habits and foods passed on by generations. These foods have been aclaimed and commercialised i.e. takeaway restaurants that play on cultural themes and cultural psych.

“We divide the main types of nutrients into macronutrients and micronutrients. Macronutrients are carbohydrates, proteins, and fat; micronutrients are vitamins and minerals, these are utilised by the mitochondria in the cells to produce energy.”

Our Food for Thought Transformation Lifestyle team of Coaches makes effective use of nutritional therapy either on its 10 weeks courses and workshops or on one-to-one sessions.

Nutritional therapy refers to dietary assessment, counselling, and support, either disease symptoms related including mental health and weight management, as well as optimal nutrition for specific goals such as sports performance, healthy pregnancy, or weight management.

Nutrition can also assist you in managing or preventing several diagnosed mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety, dementia, schizophrenia, and attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD).Furthermore, nutrition and physical activity will also prevent or assist with symptoms found in other diagnosed diseases such as Type 2 Diabetes, Coronary heart disease and some types of cancers.

Zumba

Zumba is a Cardio Dance Fitness Workout class with rhythms from all over the World.

This programme is designed for all levels and you do not need to be a dancer to participate. Just allow your body to move freely and do it at your own fitness level/pace and ability. The more you do it, the faster you learn the choreographies and the more confident you will feel. Using real dance steps taken from rhythms such as Afrobeats, Salsa, Samba, Brazilian Funk, Kizomba, Axe, Belly Dancing, and many more, these steps are combined with fitness.

Continue Reading...

The Dancing experience within these classes are exhilarating and “Mentally Stimulating”, you will feel like you are at a live show or at a party. Sexy moves, hypnotic rhythms, lots of energy and fun are the key ingredients for you to enjoy these classes. It is a react class as there is no breakdown of the steps due to its cardio based nature.

We start the class by warming up gradually, bringing our heart rate up, and then changing the speed throughout the class for a proper interval training session. At the end we finish with a cooldown session and stretches to prevent onset soreness caused by lactic acid build up in the muscles. This is a full body approach class and you can adjust the impact and the moves to your own style and physical ability. There is no judgement, we are a dance family!

The science Behind the class

Zumba is a Cardio based full body workout that incorporates interval training, which means the speed changes will help you to burn more calories. By getting your heart rate up you increase the body’s metabolism that helps you to burn unwanted fat.

This not only helps to maintain a healthy heart and good body function overall as it also boosts serotonin which is a monoamine neurotransmitter. It is a contributor to feelings of well-being and happiness. Zumba also helps to improve your coordination, strengthens your core and improves flexibility.

Dancing improves brain function and boosts memory. Several studies have shown that dancing is linked to a reduced risk of dementia. In a study carried out by researchers at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine, they found that dancing is associated with 76% reduced risk of dementia among the participants.

Improved blood pressure

A 2016 study Trusted Source involving a group of overweight women found that after a 12-week of Zumba fitness program, participants experienced a decrease in blood pressure and significant improvements in body weight.

Another 2015 study Trusted Source found a decrease in blood pressure in participants after a total of just 17 Zumba classes. The same study found that after 12 weeks of a Zumba program, participants showed a decreased heart rate and systolic blood pressure with an increase of work.These trends coincide with an increase in endurance.

Zumba is also an efficient workout in increasing aerobic capacity, a measure of cardiovascular fitness.
Intensity Level: Medium/High (flexible to individual’s fitness levels)

Martial Arts

Kapoeira & Aerobics Exercises

Afrikan people brought kapoeira to Brazil around the 16th century during the slave trade. This art has its origin in Northern Angola. It was used as a means of self-defence (body) and it was also a spiritual belief (Mind & Soul).

Continue Reading...

This form of martial art can also be described as a form of dance. This can be seen in the streets of Salvador Bahia (Brazil), people with mystical appearances gracefully showcasing their acrobatic jinga like skills & movement. Jinga or “Ginga” is the way fighters or dancers move and balance their bodies moving side to side in a 45 degrees lower to upper body movement.

The music and the movement sets the rhythm. The graceful attitude of fighters, and the lightness should not be underestimated as this is a powerful form of combat and can be one of the deadliest martial arts, with thrust, acrobacies and power kicks.Kapoeira can also be referred to as a game, is practiced in a circle, called the roda, within those who practice this martial art participate, these are called Kapoeiristas.

The instruments that are used to play the music that accompany the Kapoeira practice are berimbau, pandeiro and atabaque. The berimbau is an instrument with a single string and has AfriKan origins. This instrument drives the speed of the capoeira practice – or game as some call it – within the circle. The pandeiro is a tambourine instrument that accompanies the main beat of the capoeira music that comes from the atabaque, or the drum.

Benefits of Kapoeira

Kapoeira’s training components include strength, flexibility, stamina, speed, coordination and balance. This practice involves demanding physical and acrobatic training, it develops self-discipline and self-control, although due to the dance part of this martial art it also allows space for creativity and self-expression. Kapoeira is also beneficial for your mental health and promotes a perfect mind, body connection.

Hormones when we exercise, we produce endorphins such as dopamine and serotonin, these chemicals interact with receptors in our brain and give us a feel good and happy sensation as well as reducing stress.

Boost your self esteem: This practice can also increase your self-esteem, boost your confidence and help you to socialise more by making new friends.Kapoeira is one of the methods used by our team at FTT-Lifestyle as part of your personalised programme or within the 10 weeks course and workshops.
